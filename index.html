EBARC

The East Bay Amateur Radio Club (EBARC) in Richmond, California was founded in 1947 by a group of local amateur radio operators.

While our purpose is mainly social, club members provide a variety of services to the community including:

  • Teaching ham radio and electronics classes
  • Conducting ham radio license examinations
  • Providing emergency communications services
  • Providing radio communications for public events (e.g., walk-a-thons)

We welcome both licensed ham operators and the general public to become club members and to participate in our monthly meetings and other club activities.

Balloon Launch At East Bay Mini-Makers Faire October 27

Some club members are launching a High Altitude Balloon at the East Bay Mini-Maker Faire Sunday, October 27. The launch time is 12:00 noon. The HAB payload will include an APRS Beacon on 144.390 call sign W6MRR-11. We will also be flying a cross band repeater. Input on 145.56 MHz (162.2 Hz tone) and an output on 445.525 MHz. Program your radio now - we look forward to talking to you. You can follow the flight on the web using APRS.fi.

November Meeting: USS Hornet Radio Communications

At this meeting we will nominate our 2020 officers.

History and Hams Go Hand in Hand! Jerry Inman, AE6I, will be the featured speaker at November's club meeting. Jerry is the Radio Division lead aboard the USS Hornet (Air and Space Museum) and President of the Hornet Amateur Radio Club. Jerry will talk about the long and integral history of radio communications aboard the Hornet (the ship that launched the Doolittle Raid and picked up the Apollo astronauts). He’ll also tell us how HARC works to preserve and promote the legacy of the ship. He’ll discuss the club's current operating environment and touch on the club's Antenna Restoration Project.
