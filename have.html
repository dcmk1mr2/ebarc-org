New to amateur radio or want to do more? Here are some of the things we do on the air. What do you do?
UHF/VHF Voice

Most hams get started with a cheap handy talkie (HT - handheld or car mounted radio) . A cheap radio can be had for about $25. Great for short distance use off the grid or yacking it up long distance on a repeater. Check out our newsletter - "The Blown Fuse" for up to date information on local UHF/VHF nets.

DX'ing

If you want to talk or send information anywhere in the world this is for you. Some of our members even helped with preparation for the Heard Island DXpedition. See our links page for DX'ing organizations and Heard Island.

Emergency and Public Service

See our Current Events for opportunities to get out and use your radio to help your community.

QRP'ing

If you want to talk or send information anywhere in the world with under 10 watts this is for you. Because low power equipment is small and light it's ideal for taking on hikes and expeditions. See our links page for QRP'ing organizations.

Weak Signal Digital

Some of the most exciting new things in amateur radio are going on in digital modes. Digital modes are used from microwave down to the new LF (2200 m) and MF (630) bands. These new modes use a computer to chat, email, file transfer and even send voice. A computer sound card is used to connect the radio's audio to the computer.

There are a number of digital modes popular today which include:

  • WSJT-X is the goto program for experimenting with weak signals. The FT-8 mode is even becoming popular with contesters and DX'ers. JT-9 is still the best for very weak signal. Also popular with earth-moon-earth bounce.
  • Automatic Packet Reporting System Also known as the Automatic Position Reporting System, this system is usually used to report positions of yourself, cars and boats, even high altitude balloons. Usually found in the VHF band at 144.390 MHz but is used on other frequencies.
  • Digital voice is another fast moving area. Known for very little background noise it is available in either HF or UHF/VHF. There are a number of incompatible proprietary systems such as D-Star and System Fusion. FreeDV is an open source system that was developed for free use, mostly on HF.
  • HF Digital Text Chat - these popular mode include PSK31, RTTY, Olivia, and many others.
  • There are two microwave mesh network systems that are popular in the ham community. You can do high speed data, video, file transfer, etc. over ham microwave bands using relatively inexpensive gear intended for WiFi. Take a look at Bradband-Hamnet (HSMM-MESH) or Amateur Radio Emergency Data Network (AREDN)
  • Packet Radio is still a thing. It provides the underlying support for APRS and can be used for BBSs, file transfer, email, and chatting. Most packet radio is over UHF/VHF/HF radios with speeds ranging from 1200 to 9600 bps. Winlink Express also known as RMS Express provides email support for with support for HF/VHF/UHF. Direwolf is a popular software "soundcard" AX.25 packet modem/TNC and APRS encoder/decoder.

Satellites

Emily Clarke, N1DID, gave a presentation on amateur radio aboard satellites. Emily is on the board of AMSAT and one of the most knowledgeable people in this fascinating niche of amateur radio. Lots of information on her website and links to other sites with AMSAT info.
